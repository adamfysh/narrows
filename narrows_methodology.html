Chokepoint Dependency Simulation Model

Quantifying seaborne trade exposure
before a disruption occurs.

Narrows models bilateral trade dependency at the chokepoint level — mapping which countries import which commodities through which corridors, and calculating the financial exposure when that corridor is disrupted. The output is a defensible, scenario-specific number your model can actually use.

Methodology
How the model works
1
Trade flow reconstruction. UN Comtrade bilateral import data is matched to commodity-specific vessel classes and routed through named chokepoint corridors to build a country-pair dependency matrix.
2
Dependency scoring. Each country-commodity-chokepoint triplet is assigned a dependency percentage: the share of that import flow which transits the corridor. Scores reflect both volume and substitutability.
3
Scenario simulation. A disruption event (closure duration, capacity reduction, severity) is applied to the corridor. Exposed trade value is calculated as dependency % × annual trade value × time window.
4
Rerouting cost modelling. Alternative routes are evaluated by vessel class, detour days, bunker cost, charter rate, and cargo carrying cost — producing a per-vessel and portfolio-level overrun estimate.
5
Structured output. Results are delivered as a country-level exposure table with dependency %, value at risk, rerouting cost matrix, and fleet exposure — exportable for downstream model integration.

Scope & Limitations
What the model is and is not
Narrows produces dependency scores and exposure estimates, not price forecasts or insurance valuations. Outputs are inputs to your model, not replacements for it.
Trade flows are derived from 2023 UN Comtrade annual baselines. The model reflects structural dependency patterns, not live cargo tracking.
Rerouting cost estimates assume published vessel class benchmarks (bunker at $32k/day, Capesize charter at $18k/day). Users may substitute their own assumptions.
The dependency matrix covers 87.2% of the modelled seaborne trade universe by value. Some commodity-corridor pairs with thin bilateral data are excluded.
Results are scenario-specific. The model does not estimate probability of disruption — that assessment remains with the analyst.
